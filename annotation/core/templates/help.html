How to Play

#}

You will see a grade school math question. Then, you will be presented with a model-generated reasoning chain, one step at a time.
Your task is to decide at which step (if at all) the reasoning becomes incorrect.
If you find such a incorrect step, you will then be asked to provide three things:

  • Critique: briefly explain what's wrong, and suggest a fix in Natural Language. Please AVOID directly editing the step at this point.
  • Edit: rewrite the problematic step to fix the issue.
  • Error categorization: select the error category(ies) that best describe the error(s) in the step.
You can then submit your response. No need to look at the remaining steps!
If you have looked at all steps and haven't found an error, you can select "The entire chain is correct" at the end.
Or, if you think there is an issue that can't be localized to any step, you can select "There's a global issue" at the end.

Examples

Example 1
Question:

In a week, a tow truck pulled 10 cars for each of the first 3 days and then 4 fewer cars on each of the remaining 4 days. How many cars did it tow in total?

Model-generated reasoning chain:

✅ 1. The tow truck pulled 3 * 10 = 30 cars for the first three days.
✅ 2. On the remaining days it pulled 4 fewer cars each day.
❌ 3. That means it pulled 4 * 4 = 16 cars.

Please briefly explain what's wrong, and suggest a fix in Natural Language.

Critique:

Please rewrite this step to be correct.
